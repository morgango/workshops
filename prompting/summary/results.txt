Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system, known for its majestic appearance and swirling gases. Its immense size and gravitational pull influence the dynamics of the solar system, protecting the inner planets. Jupiter's banded atmosphere, caused by powerful jet streams, gives it a distinctive appearance. The planet has a dense core surrounded by layers of hydrogen and helium, fueling its intense magnetic field and storms. The Great Red Spot is a famous storm on Jupiter. Jupiter's diverse moons, such as Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto, offer insights into potential life beyond Earth. Jupiter has captivated humanity for centuries, inspiring myths and legends and continuing to fascinate astronomers and space enthusiasts.
